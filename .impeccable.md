# AIUB Portal+ — Design Context

A comprehensive UI/UX redesign of the American International University–Bangladesh Student Portal. Every portal page the extension touches — the top navbar, sidebar, Home, Offered Courses, Registration, Grade Reports, Curriculum, Drop Application, Financials — plus three standalone tool pages (Routine Generator, Grade Analytics, CGPA Planner) must feel like one coherent product.

## Design Context

### Users

AIUB undergraduate students (mostly BSc CSE, also BBA / Engineering) in Dhaka. They open the portal **many times per day** during the semester: registering for courses, checking class schedules, looking up grades, paying tuition, requesting drops. The native portal is slow, visually stale (Bootstrap 3 era), and important information is buried in dense tables and tiny buttons. Students are typically on a laptop at home or on campus; occasionally on mobile. Their context is *task-oriented* — find a thing, act on it, close the tab.

### Brand personality

Three words: **confident, editorial, respectful-of-time.**

- **Confident** — unapologetically opinionated about spacing, hierarchy, color. Feels like a product that someone actually designed.
- **Editorial** — display italics (Fraunces), generous line-length, left-aligned, a sense of prose rather than admin-panel anonymity.
- **Respectful-of-time** — the most important information is the largest, boldest, fastest to scan. No dense tables without summaries. No walls of equally-weighted text.

### Aesthetic direction

- **Theme**: Light. The portal is used in classrooms, libraries, and on home desks in daylight. Dark mode is not the default (students don't trade at 2am — they check grades between lectures).
- **Color spine**: royal blue (`oklch(0.42 0.18 258)` ≈ #1d4ed8) ↔ sky (`oklch(0.76 0.15 230)` ≈ #60a5fa) gradient family, with a navy ink (`oklch(0.14 0.08 260)` ≈ #0a1838) for hero backgrounds. AIUB gold (`#f59e0b` / `#fbbf24`) as the scarce accent — used on CTAs, live status dots, "unofficial extension" badges.
- **Typography**: **Fraunces** for display (headings, "live numbers", hero titles — often in italic for editorial feel) + **Geist** for body + **Geist Mono** for class IDs, grade codes, and stat numbers.
- **Hierarchy**: at least 1.25× ratio between type steps. One hero number per card. Body copy capped at ~65ch.
- **Surfaces**: white cards on a paper-tinted background (`oklch(0.98 0.006 258)`), thin blue-tinted borders, soft two-tier shadow (`0 1px 2px + 0 8px 22px -14px` of navy). No drop-shadow without elevation meaning.
- **Ink**: `#0b1a3a` (cool navy) for primary text, two tiers of muted (`#5c6987` and `#8291b1`) for metadata.
- **Important info highlighting** — this is central. Use these, and *only* these, intentionally:
  - Currently-live state → **animated gold dot** + compact badge.
  - Positive state (Ready, Synced, Unlocked, Passed) → emerald pill (`bg-emerald-100 text-emerald-800`).
  - Caution / needs-attention → amber pill + gold-tinted background band.
  - Blocking / danger → red pill on `bg-red-50`.
  - Editorial emphasis (hero CGPA, credits earned, countdown) → Fraunces display numerals at 32–48px with tabular-nums.
  - Running number updates → tabular figures always; no layout shift.

### What this is NOT

- Not cyan-on-dark. Not glassmorphism. Not rounded-icon-above-every-heading.
- Not a dashboard full of same-sized cards with the same "icon + title + number" grid pattern.
- Not gradient text (banned per impeccable's absolute bans).
- Not colored side-stripe borders on cards (banned).
- Not pure black or pure white — every neutral is tinted toward royal blue.
- Not centered-everything. Editorial means left-aligned with purposeful asymmetry.

### Cross-surface consistency

Every page the extension touches shares:

1. **The same top navbar** — navy gradient, Fraunces brand lockup with gold `+`, welcome name in Geist body.
2. **The same sidebar profile card** — navy gradient block with Fraunces name + mono student ID.
3. **The same section-block pattern** — `[counter] Title. [one-line subtitle]` on left column, content on right. Used in Routine Generator, CGPA Planner, Graph Dashboard, and soon Home's sections.
4. **The same pill + chip language** — tone=`ok|warn|err|muted` rendered identically across React and content-script CSS.
5. **The same transition timing** — `transition-colors duration-120` for hover / focus, `duration-220 ease-[cubic-bezier(0.22,1,0.36,1)]` for reveals.

### Design principles (guide every decision)

1. **Signal over chrome.** The most important thing on screen should be the biggest, boldest, most tabular. Decorative elements exist only when they clarify hierarchy.
2. **Editorial first, dashboard second.** Display italics, real line-length, prose in subtitles. Numbers are the heroes; labels are whispered.
3. **One accent per surface.** The gold / royal / emerald accents are precious. Spread them thin and they stay loud.
4. **Consistency across React + content scripts.** A student scrolling from the Home page (content-script CSS) into the Routine Generator (React + Tailwind) must feel one product, not two. The tokens file is the contract.
5. **Respect the native DOM.** Content scripts mount *onto* AIUB's Bootstrap 3. Don't rebuild what the portal already emits — restyle it. Override colors, spacing, borders; preserve structure and roles.
